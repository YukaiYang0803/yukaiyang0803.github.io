Hi there! I am Yukai Yang. I am a researcher developing reliable and interpretable machine learning methods for AI for Biology, focusing on the intersection of representation learning and uncertainty quantification.

I am currently a PhD student in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at Texas A&M University <img src='/assets/img/tamu-logo.svg' alt='TAMU' style='height:1.2em; vertical-align:middle;'>, advised by Professor [Yu Zhang](https://yuzhimanhua.github.io/) and Professor [Yang Shen](https://shen-lab.github.io/).

Previously, I worked on Uncertainty Quantification for Large Language Models, advised by Professors [Tim Rudner](https://timrudner.com/) and [Marco Morucci](https://marcomorucci.com/bio/). I also conducted research on AI Safety for multi-agent systems with Professor [Guang Cheng](http://www.stat.ucla.edu/~guangcheng/index.html)'s group at UCLA, and worked on scientific machine learning for AI4Bio with Professor [Ruqi Zhang](https://ruqizhang.github.io/). I received my M.S. in Statistics from the University of Chicago <img src='/assets/img/UChicago_Shield_2Color_Maroon_WhiteFill_RGB.svg' alt='UChicago' style='height:1.2em; vertical-align:middle;'>, and a Bachelor's degree (magna cum laude) in Data Science and Mathematics from New York University <img src='/assets/img/nyu-logo.png' alt='NYU' style='height:1.2em; vertical-align:middle;'>.
